Optimized Convolutional Neural Network-Based Bengali Speech Emotion Classifier

Published in: 2024 IEEE 21st India Council International Conference (INDICON)

Date of Conference: 19-21 December 2024

Abstract:

Speech emotion recognition (SER) is a subdomain of artificial intelligence that assists computers in comprehending user preferences and intentions. Nevertheless, the mission of SER in low-resource South-Asian languages, such as Bengali, continues to be difficult due to its content varieties, dialectic changes, acoustic variability, and speakers' ages. This work proposes a convolutional neural network-based model for SER in the Bengali language using the SUBESCO dataset. The model uses the various temporal and spectral features to recognize and understand emotions conveyed through speech. The proposed work is the first attempt to introduce augmentation to the SER task. We augment the speech signals with uniform random noise, apply time stretching and shifting, and then incorporate pitch. We compile the proposed model using Adam, Nadam, RMSProp, and SGD optimizers and tune it with hyperparameters, resulting in significantly higher accuracies compared to traditional machine-learning-based predictive models.
